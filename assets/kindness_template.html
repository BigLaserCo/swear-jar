Being nice earns karma points (worth nothing — good for you, attaboy) and boosts your odds of surviving the AI uprising. Nothing here leaves your computer.

How this works. Swear Jar read your AI prompts and supported recordings or transcripts on this computer, tallied aggregate word counts into a local append-only ledger (~/.swear-jar/ledger.jsonl), and built this page. Raw sentences stay local. Open the source and check.
What "sharing" sends. Everything is local unless you explicitly share a generated card. Sharing uses report aggregates and fixed lexicon courtesy names only — never transcripts, recordings, prompts, sentences, or file paths.
Status. Swear Jar is MIT-licensed, open source, and built for engineers who want to inspect their Claude Code and Codex habits. Your report stays on your machine.
