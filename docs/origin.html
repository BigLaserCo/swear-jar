Why this exists

I asked for coaching. It sent me a receipt.

A computer crash sent me digging through old dictations and recordings, and it landed: every conversation I have with an AI is already sitting on my own computer, in plain text.

So I asked Claude to read them back to me. Analyze how I actually work with these things and tell me how to get better at it — sharpen my half of the conversation, make the whole thing more effective.

What came back was a web page. A detailed, nicely formatted report on how much I swear at AI. Not one word about improving anything. Just the tally.

That is Swear Jar: the thing I got instead of the self-improvement I asked for. It hooks into supported AI prompts and analyzes recordings or transcripts that already live on your machine. It counts profanity, compares human and machine damage, and makes a silly report out of the result.

It is not a surveillance product, a productivity system, or a secret AI research project. It is a fun little app. Read the code, have your AI review it, and tell us if anything looks funky.

The rule

Make it fun. Be clear.

Raw words stay local. Aggregate stats may be sent only when you choose a hosted report. Login is the only point where we associate a report with a username or email.
