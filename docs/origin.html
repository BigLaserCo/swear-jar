Why this exists

Somewhere between a transcript and a f***ing gold mine.

A computer crash sent us looking through old dictation recordings and AI conversations. The obvious question followed: how frustrated do we actually get at the machines?

Swear Jar is a small, open-source answer. It hooks into supported AI prompts and analyzes recordings or transcripts that already live on your machine. It counts profanity, compares human and machine damage, and makes a silly report out of the result.

It is not a surveillance product, a productivity system, or a secret AI research project. It is a fun little app. Read the code, have your AI review it, and tell us if anything looks funky.

The rule

Make it fun. Be clear.

Raw words stay local. Aggregate stats may be sent only when you choose a hosted report. Login is the only point where we associate a report with a username or email.
