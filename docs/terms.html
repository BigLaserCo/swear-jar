Plain-language policy

We make a toy, not a mystery.

Swear Jar is a free, MIT-licensed open-source novelty app for engineers who want to inspect their Claude Code and Codex habits. Read the source or have your own AI inspect it for anything unexpected.

What stays on your machine

Raw prompts, sentences, recordings, transcripts, and code are analyzed locally. The ledger stores aggregate counts and report data, not raw text. The report is generated locally and is not remotely hosted by default.

What may be sent

Nothing leaves unless you explicitly share the generated social card or opt into the leaderboard. Those actions may send only disclosed aggregate stats: counts, totals, and censored summary data. We never send transcripts, recordings, prompts, sentences, or file paths.

Accounts

Login is optional. We associate a report with a username or email only when you choose to join an account-based feature, and may retain that information to operate the account and related features.
