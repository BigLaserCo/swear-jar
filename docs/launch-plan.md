# Swear Jar — Viral Launch Plan

## 1. POSITIONING
**One line:** "Swear Jar counts how many times you cussed out your AI — and tells you what you owe."

**The hook:** Not "I'm angry," but "look how absurdly hard I lean on this robot that I clearly love-hate." It's the confessional flex — self-deprecation as status. Everyone who does real work with AI has a private, unhinged relationship with it. Swear Jar makes that relationship *legible and funny*.

**Who shares & why:** Devs, writers, founders, "AI power users." They share because the number is a badge — it proves they're in the trenches, using AI hard enough to hate it. It's a humblebrag disguised as a confession. The privacy angle ("nothing leaves my machine, I chose to post this") lets them share a wild number without exposing a single embarrassing sentence.

## 2. OBJECTIVE RED-TEAM
Honest take: the *concept* is genuinely funny and the seed stat is strong. The risk isn't virality — it's **framing**. Get it wrong and Jim is "the guy who screams at ChatGPT."

- **"Swearing reads as toxic / anger-management."** → Reframe as the **universal AI-frustration meter**. It's not rage at a person; it's the sound of a tool that's *almost* good enough. Everyone recognizes it. Lean into shared exhaustion, never actual venom.
- **"It's a one-joke app — dead in 48h."** Real risk. → The retention hook is the *leaderboard + trend line*, not the gag. "Am I swearing more or less this month?" gives a reason to reopen. Ship the comparison ("you vs. the founder's 3,965") on day one.
- **"Privacy panic — it read my private dictation."** → Make LOCAL the loudest word in every asset. "It never phones home. You share one number, on purpose." Turn the fear into the trust flex.
- **"Founder looks unstable posting his own 3,965."** → Only works if *owned with a grin*. Jim posts it as a punchline about himself, not a boast. Self-own > flex. The 44% stat does the heavy lifting; keep the tone light.
- **"Nerd-only, no normie reach."** True, and fine — → don't chase normies. Win the AI-dev crowd hard (HN/PH/X), let the funny number leak outward. Deep beats wide for a free local tool.

**Verdict:** Ship it. The failure mode is tone, not idea. Keep it self-deprecating, keep LOCAL loud, ship the comparison feature so it's not a one-joke toy.

## 6. LAUNCH SEQUENCE
Ordered — each stacks credibility for the next.

1. **X thread (Day 0, morning).** Angle: the founder-story self-own (post E → A → D). Warmest, most shareable, seeds the number before any "buy" energy. **Metric that means it's working: replies posting their own number.** That's the meme spreading, not just likes.

2. **Show HN (Day 0, same day, ~9am ET).** Angle: "Show HN: I scanned my dictation history and found I swear at my AI 3,965 times." Technical + privacy-first (local, no cloud) is catnip here. **Metric: front-page + comments debating their own stats / the privacy model.**

3. **Product Hunt (Day 1–2).** Angle: "The dashboard for your AI rage — 100% local." Clean visuals, the leaderboard, the seed number. **Metric: top-5 of the day + share-string clicks converting to downloads.**

4. **Subreddits (Day 2–4, staggered).** r/LocalLLaMA + r/superwhisper (privacy + tool angle), r/ChatGPT + r/singularity (the funny stat). Post as a user sharing a finding, not an ad. **Metric: upvote ratio > 90% and comments with other people's numbers.**

**North-star across all four:** not downloads — **the share-string firing.** People voluntarily posting their own aggregate number is the only signal the loop is self-propelling.
